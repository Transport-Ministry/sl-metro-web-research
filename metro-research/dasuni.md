## 2. Key Features Observed

### 🔵 Singapore – TransitLink
![Singapore TransitLink Trvelguide](./screenshots/singapore-transitlink.png)
*Screenshot taken: 2025-03-09*

**Features noticed:**
- Makes traveling on MRT and buses very easy for passengers.
- Supports contactless payment through bank cards, mobile phones, or wearables.
- Users can check travel history, view past trips, and track payments in the app.
- Offers auto top-up for EZ-Link and travel cards for frequent commuters.
- Allows passengers to apply for, replace, or manage concession cards like student or senior passes.
- Provides travel claims services, notifications for promotions, and service updates.
- Lets users link multiple travel cards to one account and manage balances conveniently.
- Multilingual support (English, Chinese, Malay, Tamil)

**My observation:** The fare calculator is very user-friendly. Even a first-time visitor can easily calculate the cost of a trip and find the best route.

---

### 🔴Indonesia – jakartamrt
![TfL Homepage](./screenshots/jakartamrt.png)
*Screenshot taken: 2025-03-09*

**Features noticed:**
- Interactive metro route map with current and future lines.
- Journey planner to find trips between stations.
- Ticket information for single-trip and monthly passes.
- Real-time updates on train schedules and service alerts.
- Station facilities and accessibility information for passengers.

**My observation:** Jakarta MRT is a modern metro system in a developing country. The website clearly shows current and future lines, which helps passengers understand the system. It also provides ticketing and journey planning tools, making travel easier. The inclusion of real-time updates and station accessibility information improves commuter convenience. Even though the system is still expanding, it’s a good example of how developing countries are implementing modern metro networks.

---

### 🟠 Delhi Metro
![Tokyo Metro Homepage](./screenshots/delhi-metro.png)
*Screenshot taken: 2025-03-02*

**Features noticed:**
- Journey planner to find routes between stations
- fare calculator
- Metro network route map
- Train timetable and operating hours
- Smart card recharge information
- News, announcements, and service updates

**My observation:** The Delhi Metro website provides a lot of passenger information such as routes, fares, and smart card services. However, the homepage contains many links and sections, so the layout feels slightly more complex compared to Singapore - TransitLink

## 5. My Personal Opinion

> *Write this in your own words. What did you personally find most useful? What do you think Sri Lanka needs most?*

I think Sri Lanka’s metro website should focus on a **simplicity and mobile-first design** because many users will access it from their smartphones. Among the websites I reviewed, the Singapore TransitLink website is the most balanced because it provides useful features without making the interface complicated.

For Sri Lanka, the most important feature would be **multilingual support** so that users can easily switch between Sinhala, Tamil, and English. This will make the system accessible to both local commuters and international visitors.
